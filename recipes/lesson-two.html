Lesson two

Now that we covered some basic terminology, let's try play a five-finger scale figure.

  • Place your RH 1 (right-hand thumb) above the middle C key. Touch the key, but don't press it yet!
  • Place your other RH fingers 2345 on the following keys DEFG.
  • Now, play a scale from C up to G and back again, using fingers 1 through 5 and back to 1.

Find the C-position with your left hand also.

  • The LH 5 (pinky) goes on the C to the left of the right hand.
  • The following fingers 4321 goes on the following keys DEFG.
  • This means that the LH 1 (thumb) is on G.
  • Now, play a scale from C up to G and back, using fingers 5 through 1 and back to 5.

Due to the symmetry of the hands, when placed paralell on the keys, the fingers of the RH and LH are inversed.

This means that if we try to play the five-step C major scale in both hands simultaneously, the two hands must use different fingers.

Try to play both scales at the same time, starting on C. For each key, notice which fingers of both hands are being used.

Write down the fingering for the RH and LH when playing a five-step C major scale.
